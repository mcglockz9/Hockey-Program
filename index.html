Kadour Ziani · 7 Unlocking Postures

From Unlock Mobility — 7 Postures by Kadour Ziani. Always barefoot. Always in order — each posture prepares the next. Micro-dose protocol: 10 sec × 3 for passive holds · 3 × 10 reps for active/dynamic. Pain must not exceed 3–4/10. Active/Dynamic versions BEFORE exertion (warm-up); Passive/Static AFTER exertion (cool-down). The big toe is the common foundation of all 7. Full sequence on Day 3; select postures integrated on all other days.

5 FOUNDERS: Cat · Cow · Camel · Grasshopper · Pigeon 2 CHALLENGES: Frog · Gorilla
Posture 1 · FOUNDER

The Cat — Body Awakening

"Mother of postures." Awakens and lubricates all basic joints of the musculoskeletal system. Opens every session.

  • Passive: Lie on side. Take knee in hollow of forearm, lift bent leg to chest. With other hand, grab all toes and bend toward you. Heel stays close to butt.
  • Active (standing): Place foot on box at knee height. Add kettlebell, perform split squat on standing leg.
  • Alternate: Seated on floor with foot lifted; or standing with foot on chair.
⏱ Passive: 10 sec × 3 each side · Active: 3 × 10 reps · BEFORE training
Posture 2 · FOUNDER

The Cow — Humility Check Up

Seated between flared heels — NOT on heels. Axial alignment of thighs. Seen in seiza (Japan), Berber prayer, and Asian deep squat cultures.

  • Passive: Sit on ground with buttocks between heels (heels open wide outward creating a hollow). Fists on ground in front, thumbs extended outward. Spine tall.
  • Active: Lean to one side, opposite hand holds weight. 10 sec × 3 each side.
  • Dynamic: Push-ups from fist position. 10 reps × 3.
⏱ Passive: 10 sec × 3 · Active: 3 × 10 reps · BEFORE training
Posture 3 · CHALLENGE

The Frog — Foetal Rebirth

Most adults cannot do this — the first real test. Increases running speed, pushing force, vertical leap, and stride length.

  • Passive: Standing, feet pointing FORWARD, descend into complete deep squat with HEELS FLAT. Gap between legs proportional to shoulders. Apply armpits/elbows on roundness of knees. Arms relaxed in front.
  • Active: Same position holding weights in each hand. 10 sec × 3.
  • Dynamic: Deep squat up/down with weights. 10 reps × 3. Add ankle flexions between reps.
⏱ Passive: 10 sec × 3 · Pain max 3–4/10 · AFTER training
Posture 4 · FOUNDER

The Camel — Reload

Ziani's favorite. Half COW + half FROG. The most versatile posture — bridges the two families.

  • Passive: Start from COW position. Place one fist on ground (thumb extended). With other hand, grab knee and raise it — bring foot flat on ground with knee pressed against collarbone/chest. Hold 10 sec × 3 each side.
  • Active: Same position with weights hanging from hands.
  • Dynamic: Single-leg squats (3 × 10) pushing with outer face of the ankle you were sitting on.
⏱ Passive: 10 sec × 3 each side · Dynamic: 3 × 10 · BEFORE training
Posture 5 · FOUNDER

The Grasshopper — The Missing Link

"Big toe is the governor of the body — a rudder from which movements are decided, engaged and oriented." The cornerstone of human mobility.

  • Passive: From COW position, grasp shin at malleolus (ankle bone). Lift foot up so leg straightens. The arch rests on FIVE TOES bent toward the knees (extreme toe extension). Hold 10 sec × 3 each side.
  • Active: Apply palm against heel of upright foot; or hold weight above thigh; or lean forward pressing shoulder on knee.
  • Dynamic: Front-and-back swings from foot to bent toes; or rise on knees and sit on ankle.
  • Cultural reference: Islamic tashahoud seated prayer position.
⏱ Passive: 10 sec × 3 each side · Dynamic: 3 × 10 · BEFORE training
Posture 6 · FOUNDER

The Pigeon — The Virtuous Triangle

FIRST posture with ROTARY/circular movements (all previous are linear). Crucial for pelvis opening and hip release. Weights mandatory for active version.

  • Passive: From Grasshopper base, lean on fist (thumb extended), lift buttocks, slide foot along opposite thigh until toes reach edge of the opposite knee — creating a TRIANGLE with the leg. Sit down. Hands on thighs. Hold 10 sec × 3 each side.
  • Variations: (a) Small trunk rotation toward triangle thigh, lower trunk onto thigh. (b) Tilt trunk to side, place shoulder on knee.
  • Active: Same positions with weights — WEIGHTS MANDATORY.
  • Dynamic: Ascent/descent movements from base variations.
⏱ Passive: 10 sec × 3 each side · AFTER training (rotary — static)
Posture 7 · MASTERY CHALLENGE

The Gorilla — The Harmony

"As a rainbow brings together its colors." Unifies all previous postures — the harmony of strength and flexibility. Used by NBA stars Nicolas Batum and Frank Ntilikina.

  • Builds directly on PIGEON base. Add second fist on ground between the two legs (previously only one fist). Rise up on BOTH fists, thumbs extended outward throughout.
  • Thumbs must be detached along entire length — they serve as balance points, absorb stress, and protect carpal canals.
  • Active: Hold raised on both fists. 10 sec × 3 each side.
  • Dynamic: Push-ups on fists (10 pumps × 3). Big toe bent is KEY support throughout.
⏱ Active: 10 sec × 3 · Dynamic: 3 × 10 push-ups · BEFORE training

Key Exercise Illustrations

Critical movement patterns referenced throughout the program. Study the positions and cues before adding load.

Trap Bar Deadlift

Day 1 · A1 · 4×5 @ 80–85%
3 sec eccentric. Hip hinge, not squat. Neutral spine — do not round lower back.

Bulgarian Split Squat

Day 1 · B1 · 3×8 each leg @ 70%
Rear foot on Bosu ball. Unstable surface amplifies neuromuscular demand.

Hang Power Clean

Day 4 · A1 · 4×4 @ 75–80%
Triple extension of ankle/knee/hip replicates skating push-off. Key power movement.

Reactive Lateral Bound

Day 1 · A2 · 4×4 each leg
Contrast after deadlift. Land and absorb — 2 sec hold. Trains single-leg deceleration.

Pallof Press Iso Hold

Day 2 · C2 · 3×20 sec each side
Anti-rotation core stability. Direct transfer to puck protection and contact strength.

Skater Squat (TG Method)

Day 1 · C · 3×10 each leg
Mimics skating push-off angle. 2 sec isometric hold at bottom. Bodyweight to loaded.
